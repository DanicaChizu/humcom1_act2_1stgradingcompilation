INFORMATION SYSTEM

COMPUTER:
Computer is an electronic device that accepts data as an input, processes the data and it produces information as output.

INFORMATION SYSTEMS:
Information Systems is an empirical study of systems with a particular reference to information and complementary hardware and software networks used by individuals and organizations to collect, filter, process, produce and also distribute information.

COMPUTER HARDWARE:
Computer Hardware includes the physical parts of a computer, such as the case, CPU(Central Processing unit), monitor, mouse, keyboard, speaker, motherboard and etc,.

COMPUTER SOFTWARE:
Computer Software are the programs and applications that tells instructions to the computer. It comrpises the entire set of programs, procedures and routines associated with the operation of a computer system.

DATABASE:
Database is an ordered collection of structured records, or data, usually stored in a computer system electronically. It is then possible to easily access, handle, change, update, monitor, and organize the data. For the writing and querying of data, most databases use structured query language (SQL).

SYSTEM DEVELOPMENT LIFE CYCLE:
System Development Life Cycle (SDLC) is also referred to as the Application Development Life Cycle. A process for planing, creating, testing, and deploying an information system.

NETWORKS:
In order to share resources (such as printers and CDs), exchange files or enable electronic communications, the network consists of two or more computers which are connected. Via wires, telephone lines, radio waves, satellites, or infrared light beams, computers on a network may be linked.

INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY:
Information Technology (IT) is the use of computers to store, retrieve, transmit, and manipulate data or information.

INFORMATION MANAGEMENT:
Information Management (IM) concerns a cycle of organizational activity.

COMPUTER INFORMATION SYSTEM(S) (CIS)
Computer Information is an area in which computers and algorithmic processes are studied, including their concepts, software and hardware designs, implementations, and social effects, while IS emphasizes functionality over design.
